Leadership Message

“Black September” of 2008, will go down in the annals of financial history. Within a matter of 2-3 weeks there were historic bankruptcies, stock market crashes & severe loss of confidence in the global financial industry. This saga started several years back with the so-called US Housing & Home mortgages. Who borrowed? How much was borrowed and who funded them - were the key actors in this saga. Many mortgage loans were securitized, meaning they were pooled together in securities issued that were sold around the world. Once the mortgage loans declined in value due to delinquencies and foreclosures, the securities also fell in value. This created problems for the banks and people in other countries who had purchased these securities. Securitization of home mortgages can therefore lead to problems in countries where the homes are not even located.

Key villain was the “subprime”. Subprime mortgages are mortgages that are extended to less creditworthy individuals to enable more people to purchase homes. However, being less creditworthy, there are likely to be more problems with these types of mortgages. Secondly once these were securitized – these became the “toxic cocktail”. Subprime mortgages accounted for more than 50 percent of all foreclosures in USA. Some US Housing metrics - 80 Million Homes, 53 million mortgages – of which 48 million are paying on time & 5 million are behind. This amounts to only 9.2% of 53 Million with 2.8% in foreclosure. These figures do not look menacing – in terms of the math, however United States being so big economically and financially, it undoubtedly affected other countries given the globalization & trade that exists with USA.

The US government decided to selectively bail out some financial institutions, but not all. For example - Bear Stearns was bailed out but not Lehman Bros. In hindsight, this may not have been the best decision!!!

What is the cumulative damage & counting as we move into the future? Losses, write-downs, capital raised & jobs being cut by financial institutions worldwide. Countries & governments are seriously reviewing & pondering to what level of foreclosures are appropriate relative to providing more credit for more people to purchase homes.

Jyotee Singh
Industry Leader, Asia Pacific & Canada
